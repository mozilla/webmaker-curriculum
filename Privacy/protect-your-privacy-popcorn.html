Activity

Protect Your Privacy

Participants to critically examine a video to point out privacy mistakes and correct them using Popcorn Maker.

Made by Dumitru Gherman, Emma Irwin and Laura Hilliger

Steps for the Activity

  1. Demonstrate how to use Popcorn Maker. Tell participants that they will be hacking a video. You can use the tutorial video here if you wish. This video gives an introduction to Popcorn Maker and shows the learner how to use the interface.

  2. Ask participants to find five unsafe practices and describe their consequences by remixing this video with Popcorn Maker.
  3. Suggested themes:
    • Don't leave passwords in open spaces.
    • Protect your personal information.
    • Don't leave your computer unlocked.
    • Secure ALL devices with unique and secure passwords.
    • Don't click on emails, or links from people you don't know, or that solicit personal information.

Materials

  • Projector
  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Popcorn Tips and Tricks
  • Popcorn Maker support articles

Discussion

At the end of the session, have participants present their new videos, explain their work process and what they learned. Talk about the the privacy themes they noticed during the activity and give constructive feedback on their videos.
