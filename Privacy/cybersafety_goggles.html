Activity

Remix for Cybersafety with the Goggles

The goal of this activity is for participants to explore privacy best practices through remix.

Made by Dumitru Gherman, Emma Irwin and Laura Hilliger

Steps for the Activity

  1. If you haven’t already, install the Goggles here. Tell participants that they will be hacking Thomas Tabby's Facebook profile to make it safe using these best practices. Go to Thomas Tabby's profile and turn on the Goggles. You can do this by clicking on the bookmarklet.

  2. Demonstrate how to use the Goggles, slowly hover the cursor over objects so that participants can see that the tool is inspecting the code and showing the object tags for each object. Point out the tags, attributes and elements. Find the object tags that you want to change or replace (e.g. the p tag or img tag).

  3. Hit “R” on your keyboard or just click on an element. Show participants that the code is revealed. Ask participants for help hacking the code.

  4. After you have made the changes, hit the "Save Changes" button and show everyone the hack. Now, show everyone how to do this step by step.

  5. Suggested themes:
    • Don't share any photo publicly, you wouldn't share with your grandma.
    • Never disclose your whereabouts publicly, or disclose you are alone.
    • Respect the privacy of others, don't post or tag photos of people without their permission.
    • Do not meet, or give out your full name, address, or phone number to anyone online that you don't trust or know in person.
    • Never accept friend requests, or interact with strangers online. Understand that how people portray themselves might not necessarily be true.
    • Do be positive, enjoy sharing and making things made possible by the web.

Materials

Discussion

At the end of the session, have participants present their new webpages, explain their work process and what they learned. Talk about the process of making Tabby's profile more private.

Go over the steps again.

Look at the code, point out the tags, attributes and elements.

Hack one part of the webpage. Hit the “save changes” button to save changes locally on your computer.

Share: hit “P” to publish the new webpage on the Internet. Take a screenshot or copy and paste the code into a HTML document.
