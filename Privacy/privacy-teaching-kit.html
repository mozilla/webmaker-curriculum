Teaching Kit

Privacy and Security Teaching Kit

This Teaching Kit is a set of hands-on learning activities for teaching online Security and Privacy

Made by Laura Hilliger, Dumitru Gherman, Emma Irwin, Ben Moskowitz, Atul Varma, Alina Hua,
Stacy Martin, Matthew Willse, and Shane Caraveo

Description

We all love the web. It can be a magical, collaborative space with endless possibilities for creativity, connection, problem-solving and access to knowledge.

BUT, the same openness and sharing that make the Web great also mean we need to educate ourselves about protecting our privacy and security online. As recent headlines around the world reveal, educating citizens to protect their online privacy has never been more important -- and is now as crucial to web literacy as using a browser or operating a mouse.

This Teaching Kit includes hands-on activities for helping participants learn basic privacy practices quickly. They're designed to help people think about their own privacy, and be able to describe in their own words why privacy is important to them.

With the final activity, you'll create a"Wizard of Oz" moment that pulls back the curtain. Using a free and open source tool called Mozilla Lightbeam, you can help visually show learners how cookies and third-party trackers are monitoring them as they perform their own everyday tasks on the web. It paints a personalized picture of each participant's own data trail, and what can be done to help manage and protect it.

Learning objectives

Learners will understand:

  • the importance of establishing good passwords
  • the kind of information they should never disclose online
  • how to respect others' privacy
  • software that can help protect their privacy on the web
  • what cookies are and why they are used
  • that there are third parties who may be tracking what they do online
  • the importance of securing your devices

Learners will be able to :

  • create secure passwords
  • take basic steps to protect their online identities
  • detect online scams and phishing techniques
  • remix a webpage or video to explore the issue of privacy
  • install browser add-ons that help protect their privacy
  • change the default behavior of websites, add-ons and extensions to make browsing more secure
  • identify and take steps to keep elements of their identity private
  • understand the difference between http and https

What you'll make together

Participants will remix an online profile page to implement privacy best practices. They'll also remix a video to delve deeper into common mistakes surrounding privacy practices, and install Mozilla Lightbeam to learn about tracking on the web.

Preparation

Ensure that computers have the latest version of the Firefox or Chrome browser. It might be helpful to have learners set up a Mozilla Webmaker account before the session, so that they can save their work.

Discussion Questions

  • What does protecting your privacy mean?
  • Why is it important to protect your identity online?
  • How should you protect your data on the web?
  • How can you manage unique passwords?
  • What is a cookie?
  • Why should you change default settings in your browser or on websites?
  • How can you protect the privacy of others?

Assessment criteria

Did the learner demonstrate an ability to:
  • share their thoughts about privacy and security on the Web?
  • engage in a discussion about privacy with peers?
  • create and remember a secure password?
  • switch on private browsing?
  • install a browser add-on?
  • remix media to display an understanding of key privacy concepts?
  • use a search engine to find specific content?
  • visit suggested websites for resources?
  • demonstrate creativity?
  • work well with others?

Agenda
