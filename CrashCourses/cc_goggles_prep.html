How to Begin with the Goggles

Background and Set Up

What this page is

Get your feet wet in webmaking as you prepare to use the TASCasaurus Kit or the XRay Googles Kit, both of which use the X-Ray Goggles to help learners make their first hacks.

Familiarize yourself with relevant terms.

  1. HTML

    HTML (Hyper Text Markup Language) is the language used to tell computers how to structure webpages. HTML stands for Hyper Text Markup Language. Here’s a really simple web page:

    I can <em>hear</em> you!

    Because the word is wrapped in what we call tags, this page displays as a single line of text with the word “hear” italicized. The <em> tag tells the computer to emphasize anything inside of it. If a blind person were listening to the webpage, a computerized voice would place emphasis on the word “hear”.

  2. Hacking

    In this context, hacking refers to remixing content to make new things for the web. Hacking is creative and collaborative. It is not used here to refer to actions that are malicious or illegal.

Update your browsers

Access a computer with high-speed Internet and check to make sure it has the latest version of one of the browsers (to download a browser, click on the link and follow the directions).

  1. Firefox (Mac or PC): http://www.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/new/
  2. Google Chrome (Mac or PC): https://www.google.com/intl/en/chrome/browser/
  3. Safari (Mac): http://safari.soft32.com/free-download?gclid=CPHwkrqntbECFYtV4god0AUAPQ

Install the XRay Goggles

  1. Go to: http://hackasaurus.org/en-US/goggles/
  2. Try the first hack as shown (optional, but fun!)
  3. Scroll down and look for the word “Install“ on the left (you may have to hit “Esc” to turn off the goggles first)
  4. Click the link and follow it to an installation page. From there, click on the video that shows you how to add the bookmark bar to your browser and follow directions
