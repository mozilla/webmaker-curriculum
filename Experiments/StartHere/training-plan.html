Tip Sheet

Planning a Training

This guide will help you plan, promote and document your ideas, and help you run a successful training. It's an expansion of the wonderful Medium-sized Event Guide.

Made by Laura Hilliger

Before the Event

  1. Find collaborators. Find people to work with who you find interesting. They can help you facilitate, promote, document and share your experience.

  2. Create an list of people to invite. Teaching people who want to teach means finding people who want to teach! Create a list of people who might be interested.

  3. Pick a date. Setting a date will push you to get your planning and preparation finished. How about 3 weeks from tomorrow?

  4. Find a space. Secure a comfortable area where you can sit together in a circle. For ideas on spaces, check out the Starting a Club tip sheet. Check if there is internet; although, you don't even need it to teach the web.

    Choosing your date might be dependent on when your venue is available, so it's probably best to choose a date and a venue at about the same time.

  5. Determine your goals. Use broadstrokes in the beginning to figure out what you want to teach people during your training workshop. Later, you'll gather input from your potential learners, so just keep in mind the themes you want to cover.

  6. Promote and Invite. If your training is an open, public event, think about your target participants. What are they interested in? How can you approach them? Make invitations tailored to their interests.

    You should consider writing a blog post as well as sending personalized emails to people you want to make sure are able to come. Add your event to the Webmaker site and share your event across the web.

  7. Set up a method for feedback. Before your event, schedule a call with participants and ask them what they want to learn. Talk to them about what you want to teach, and think about how you can accomplish both sets of goals.

  8. Craft an Agenda. Once you know what you want to teach and what your potential participants want to learn, begin crafting an agenda. Depending on your goals, there are lots of excellent agendas you can make your own at Webmaker Training. Below are more suggestions for crafting an agenda.

  9. Have people register. Send a follow up email to people you've invited and ask them to RSVP for your event. You'll want to know exactly how many people are coming so you can make sure you have enough facilitators, mentors, food and so forth.

  10. Gather materials. Gather paper, pens, computers, post-its, funny hats, markers and anything else you need, think about snacks and beverages and make sure you have all the things you need for a comfortable and informative training workshop.

    During the Event

  11. Welcome and warm up. Gather the group in a circle and invite them to introduce themselves. Maybe throw in a fun game to warm up. Give your participants an overview of the day.

    • Recommended time: 30 minutes
    • Choose a couple ideas for warming up: Facilitate a go-around so that people can learn each other's names. Then, discuss controversial statements in an interactive Spectrogram, dance together with Ali Baba and the 40 Thieves or roleplay how to teach in pairs.

  12. Remix your own agenda. Break into small groups of 3 - 5 participants each. Invite participants to explore the Web Literacy Map and choose topics they would like to learn more about. Have learners write down the learning objectives they want to cover, this will help you see if your agenda needs to be modified.

    • Recommended time: 40 minutes
    • Other activities to get started: Learn how to Make Your First Web Page, Make a Movie Poster or Make a Meme. Or just encourage participants to browse the Web Literacy Map and think about what they'd like to accomplish during your workshop.

  13. Make things. Have learners work together in small groups to plan their own learning events. You can even have learners build their own curriculum and agendas.

  14. Share what you made. Have participants show each other what they made and talk about their plans. Add photos and links to your event page. Ask for everyone's contact information so you can stay in touch. Wrap up with thank-yous and how to stay connected with the Webmaker community.

Details

  • 10 - 50 participants
  • 5+ hours duration, Preparation needed
  • At a school, library or welcoming space

Materials (optional)

  • Computer(s)
  • Internet
  • Pens and paper
