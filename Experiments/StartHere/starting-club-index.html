Tip Sheet

Tips and Tricks for Starting a Club

Using these pro-tips, insights, and examples, spend some time planning and thinking about how to facilitate learning in an informal Webmaker club. Then get going and #teachtheweb!

Made by Laura.

Collaborate

People = Fun

Find people to work with who you find interesting. They can help you plan, facilitate, promote, document and share your experience.

Coordinate

Dates, Space, Stuff and People

Pick your first date and find a cafe, library, or hackerspace where you can be comfortable and move chairs around. Figure out what you need (food, drinks, post its, computers, etc), and list people you want to invite.

Deliberate

Agenda and Feedback

Figure out what you want to teach. Gather input from potential learners, and keep in mind the themes you want to cover. Find agendas here.

Advocate

Online and Locally

Share your club plans on social media, set up a web presence, define a hashtag, post flyers and tell any and everyone about your club both online and in your local community.
