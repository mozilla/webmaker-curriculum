Remix Context: with Popcorn Maker

The goal of this activity is for learners to remix the context of a video by adding pop ups and new commentary.

Made by Laura Hilliger

Steps for the Activity

  1. If you havenâ€™t already, visit Popcorn Maker. Tell participants that they will be remixing a video by adding popups that change the context of the video. You might want to give specific guidance on how they should change the context. For example, ask them to create popups that take a political position on what happens in the video. Alternatively, you might ask them to create a commentary on gender bias in the video. If you need an introduction to Popcorn Maker, check out the Popcorn Maker support articles.

  2. Demo the Popcorn Maker interface, then allow learners to work individually or together. Have learners find a commercial they would like to comment on on YouTube or use an HTML5 video from anywhere on the web.

    The Prelinger Archives has lots of videos, and some even include an "OGG" file, an open video format that works with Popcorn Maker. Once learners have found a video to work with, have them copy the URL and bring it into Popcorn Maker.

  3. Float around to help learners that get stuck and encourage them to help each other. In the next activity, learners will have a specific task to complete, so for now, allow them to play with Popcorn, get used to the interface, and change the context of the video however they choose.

  4. Have learners publish their projects and post them to a blog or save links in an Etherpad.

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Projector
  • Etherpad
  • Tips and Tricks for Popcorn Maker

Discussion

Have participants talk about their films. Ask them what they had to do to make a story of their own. Reinforce the idea that Popcorn allows them to pull in dynamic data and change context. Ask learners how their film could influence someone's opinion.

Following the presentations, facilitators should direct participants to other resources in their community or online to learn more about storytelling, user participation and interactivity. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
