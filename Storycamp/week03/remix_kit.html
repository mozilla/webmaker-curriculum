What is this kit all about?

Through the activities in this Kit, learners will explore remixing of both content and context. They will formulate opinions and commentary to layer on top of video, sound and images using Popcorn Maker. Additionally, they will explore various social issues in their neighborhoods and in advertising.

Learning objectives

Learners will understand:

  • Using data allows you to change the context of a story.
  • Remixing is a form of derivative artwork.
  • the open structure of the Web allows you to remix almost anything.
  • Using data allows you to change the context of a story.
  • A mashup is putting together two pieces of content to create new meaning.
  • Gender stereotyping is assigning general traits to a particular sex

Learners will be able to :

  • Use Popcorn Maker to pull dynamic data from the web into a video.
  • Recontextualize a story by adding data to it.
  • Use keyboard shortcuts (i.e. for copying and pasting)
  • Right-click and scroll with the mouse
  • Create storyboards and schematics.

What you'll make together

Learners will use Popcorn Maker to make a creative commentary and report on their neighborhoods.

Preparation

Make sure computers have the latest version of Firefox or Chrome on them. Help learners set up a Persona account before the session, so that they can save their work.

Discussion Questions

  • What is remixing?
  • How can you remix an image? Text? A video? How can you remix a physical space?
  • How do mashups change the meaning of things?
  • How did feedback help you iterate and improve your project?
  • Why is it important to comment on other people's work, ideas or commentary?

Assessment criteria

Did the learner show their ability to:
  • understand and use recontextualization
  • understand and use Popcorn
  • find content to remix and reuse
  • change the story of a piece of media by adding information to it
  • use a search engine to find specific content
  • find the content he/she was looking for
  • use creativity
  • work with others
